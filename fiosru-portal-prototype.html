Fiosrú, Office of the Police Ombudsman

Fiosrú is the statutory body which deals with complaints from members of the public about the Gardaí.

New — online complaint portal

Make your complaint online, and follow it at every stage

You can now submit a complaint to Fiosrú online and track it from admissibility through to a decision — what we call the Confidence Thread. Niamh, our digital assistant, can guide you, or check whether your complaint is within the 12-month time limit.

  • Acknowledged quickly, with what happens next explained
  • Tracked at every stage with your reference
  • Held securely on EEA-resident infrastructure

Independent oversight you can trust

Fiosrú deals with complaints from the public where they have concerns about gardaí. If something happened to you, or you witnessed it, you can make a complaint — and follow it at every stage.

Step 1 of 5 About you

About you

So we can keep you updated. You can ask to remain anonymous to the Garda member concerned later in the process.

You can make a complaint on behalf of someone else. If you’re in immediate danger, contact the Gardaí on 112 or 999.

When and where

There’s normally a 12-month time limit for making a complaint. If you’re outside it, we may still be able to help — tell us anyway.

What happened

In your own words. Include anything you think matters — what was said or done, and how it affected you.

Who was involved

Any detail helps us identify the right people — but don’t worry if you don’t know everything.

You can request anonymity. We’ll discuss what can be shared and what can be protected as your complaint progresses.

Have a quick look. You can change anything before you send it.
